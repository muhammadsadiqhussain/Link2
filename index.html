Exploring the World of Literature.

Classic novels have had a profound impact on literature, shaping its themes, structures, and styles while providing a foundation for modern storytelling. Works like Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen delve into love, social class, and the complexities of human relationships, redefining romantic fiction with its wit and exploration of societal norms. Moby-Dick by Herman Melville explores the themes of obsession, revenge, and humanity's struggle against nature, offering a deeply symbolic narrative. Similarly, To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee examines racial injustice, moral courage, and the loss of innocence, becoming a cornerstone for socially conscious literature. These timeless books not only influenced countless writers but also enriched readers' understanding of universal truths, ensuring their enduring relevance.
